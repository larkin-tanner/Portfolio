About

Driven by Innovation

I'm an electrical engineering student with a passion for designing systems that solve real world problems. My work spans power systems, embedded electronics, and sustainable energy solutions.

The electrical engineering label doesn't stop me from pursuing other forms of problem solving. Whether it's desigining systems, testing sensors, designing circuitboards, working on guitar pedals, repairing broken instruments, building surfboards from scratch, or machining and 3D printing things I didn't know I needed, I'm always having a great time problem-solving

No matter how big or small the problem, I believe in an enthusiastic hands on approach and an open mind can lead to innovative solutions.

Beyond Engineering

Aside from work and school, my passions extend into activities that keep me constantly excited. Click into each photo for more!

As a lifelong surfer, I look for waves at every opportunity, travelling miles across the northeast, the country, and even to other continents for waves. This is, when I'm not shaping boards or doing other boardsports.
It wasn't until I got accepted to BU and joined the snowboard team that I fell in love with snowboarding. As President of the Club now, I spend my weekends in winter travelling to competitions across the northeast, and regionals and nationals competitions each year, going to New York, California, and Oregon to compete at a national level. I have won several gold medals at the regionals stage as an individual and as a team, showing me the importance of being a good leader and coach.
I began skateboarding at 5 years old following in my dad's footsteps. It's certainly the most accessible of my hobbies in Boston's urban environment. Skateboarding turns what would be a mundane expedition into an adventure or a mental battle depending on the circumstances. I spend my time filming full length skateboarding videos with friends and compete each year in local competitions.
While I am still new to biking, it has been a great way to explore the city and Massachusetts. Bikepacking to Provincetown and back this summer was a huge accomplishment I am very proud of.
I have been a guitarist since I was 10 years old, and since then I have been a part of multiple bands, playing locally, going on tour, recording professionally, creating merch, selling records, and constantly adapting to new environments.
Everywhere I go, I enjoy being outside and pushing my body to its limit, when not participating in any other hobbies that allow me to do that, hiking and exploring new areas is a great way to do so.
